CHINA:CASUAL MEETING

Ni chi le ma? or 'Have you eaten?' is a very common form of greeting in China and not to be taken literally. It's intent is similar to being asked 'how's it going?' or 'what new?'

In the Chinese culture, if someone is making the introductions, to introduce your self is considered disrespectful. So when it is your turn to be introduced, stand up, smile and look at the people also being introduced with ease. After being introduced, you can shake hands with each other and give mutual greetings

In China, there are many strict conventional rules on introduction to others:

    • a. The junior should be introduced to the senior first;
      b. The male should be introduced to the female first;
      c. The inferior should be introduced to the superior first;
      d. The host should be introduced to the guest first.
