JAPAN : Birthday Party

If you are invited AND you decide to go, bring a present! Yes, no matter how small or cheap it is. Or else you will be in awkuard situation because everyone else will have some kind of present for the birthday person.

Birthday party in Japan is usually held at someone (usually birthday person's) house. Remember that you will have to take off shoes if you are going to a Japanese house. Don't wear socks that has holes!

Birthday party isn't really a party. It's an occation where friends gather together, eat and have fun together. So don't wear too sexy or too formal clothes -- these are for dance parties.

One taboo question: "How much was that/this present?" Japanese are really careful not to talk about money related topics, because it might offend someone or might sound like you are showing off by accident.

Lastly -- Birthday party is for the BIRTHDAY PERSON. You are not on the center of the stage on that day. Be humble and don't show off.
